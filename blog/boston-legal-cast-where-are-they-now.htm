Boston Legal Cast: Where Are They Now?

Home / Blog

Few legal dramas have left as lasting an impression as Boston Legal. With its sharp wit, unforgettable characters, and socially charged storylines, the show remains a fan favorite even years after its finale. But what happened to the brilliant cast after the show ended? More importantly, how have their careers—and personal lives—intersected with today’s most pressing global issues? Let’s take a deep dive into where the stars of Boston Legal are now and how they’ve engaged with modern challenges like climate change, political polarization, and social justice.

James Spader (Alan Shore)

Career Highlights Post-Boston Legal

James Spader’s portrayal of the morally ambiguous yet charming Alan Shore earned him three Emmys. After the show ended, Spader took on another iconic role: Raymond "Red" Reddington in The Blacklist. The NBC series has been a massive success, running for nearly a decade.

Activism and Current Projects

Spader has kept a relatively low profile when it comes to activism, but his choice of roles often reflects societal tensions. The Blacklist frequently tackles themes of government corruption and surveillance—topics that resonate in today’s era of whistleblowers and cybersecurity debates. Off-screen, Spader has quietly supported environmental causes, though he rarely makes public statements.

William Shatner (Denny Crane)

From Denny Crane to Space Travel

William Shatner, already a legend thanks to Star Trek, brought Denny Crane’s eccentric brilliance to life. Post-Boston Legal, Shatner continued acting but also ventured into space tourism—literally. In 2021, he became the oldest person to travel to space aboard Blue Origin’s New Shepard rocket.

Climate Advocacy and Public Commentary

Shatner’s space journey gave him a unique perspective on climate change. He’s since spoken passionately about Earth’s fragility, urging action against global warming. He’s also been vocal about political extremism, using his platform to call for unity in an increasingly divided America.

Candice Bergen (Shirley Schmidt)

A Return to Comedy and Political Satire

After Boston Legal, Candice Bergen reprised her role as Murphy Brown in a 2018 revival, tackling modern media and political dysfunction. The show’s sharp critique of fake news and Trump-era politics made it both controversial and relevant.

Women’s Rights and Representation

Bergen has long been an advocate for gender equality. In interviews, she’s criticized Hollywood’s lingering gender pay gap and the underrepresentation of older women in leading roles. Her career choices—like Murphy Brown’s comeback—reflect her commitment to challenging stereotypes.

Rene Auberjonois (Paul Lewiston)

A Legacy Beyond Boston Legal

The late Rene Auberjonois, who passed away in 2019, had a prolific career spanning theater, film, and TV. Post-Boston Legal, he voiced characters in animated hits like Archer and Star Wars: The Clone Wars.

Remembering His Advocacy

Auberjonois was a staunch supporter of the arts and LGBTQ+ rights. His work often highlighted marginalized voices, and his passing was mourned by fans who admired both his talent and his activism.

Julie Bowen (Denise Bauer)

From Law to Modern Family

Julie Bowen’s career skyrocketed after Boston Legal when she landed the role of Claire Dunphy in Modern Family. The sitcom’s success made her a household name.

Mental Health and Parenting in the Digital Age

Bowen has used her platform to discuss mental health, particularly the pressures of parenting in the social media era. She’s been candid about her struggles with anxiety, helping destigmatize mental health discussions.

Mark Valley (Brad Chase)

Action Roles and Military Themes

Mark Valley transitioned to action-heavy roles post-Boston Legal, including Human Target and Body of Proof. His work often explores military and law enforcement themes—timely given today’s debates over policing and veterans’ rights.

Supporting Veterans

Off-screen, Valley has supported organizations aiding veterans, reflecting his on-screen roles’ themes.

Monica Potter (Lori Colson)

Entrepreneurship and Health Advocacy

After Boston Legal, Monica Potter starred in Parenthood but later shifted focus to her business, Pure Joy by Monica Potter, a skincare line promoting clean beauty.

Cancer Awareness

Potter is a breast cancer survivor and has become a vocal advocate for early detection and women’s health.

Final Thoughts

The Boston Legal cast didn’t just move on to new projects—they engaged with the world’s biggest issues, from climate change to social justice. Their careers and activism prove that the show’s legacy isn’t just in its scripts but in how its stars continue to shape conversations today.
