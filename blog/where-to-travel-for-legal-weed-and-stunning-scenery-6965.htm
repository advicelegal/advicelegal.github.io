Where to Travel for Legal Weed and Stunning Scenery

Home / Blog

The intersection of legal cannabis and breathtaking landscapes is a dream come true for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure. As more countries and states embrace marijuana legalization, a new wave of cannabis tourism has emerged. Whether you're a nature lover, a photography enthusiast, or just someone who enjoys a laid-back vibe with a view, these destinations offer the perfect blend of legal weed and jaw-dropping scenery.

The Best Destinations for Cannabis and Nature

1. Denver, Colorado – The Rocky Mountain High

Colorado was one of the first U.S. states to legalize recreational cannabis, and Denver remains a top destination for weed enthusiasts. But beyond the dispensaries, the real magic lies in the Rocky Mountains.

Why Visit?

  • Scenic Hikes & National Parks: Explore Rocky Mountain National Park, where alpine lakes, towering peaks, and wildlife sightings are abundant.
  • 420-Friendly Lodging: Many cabins and Airbnbs allow cannabis consumption, so you can wake up to mountain views with a joint in hand.
  • Red Rocks Amphitheatre: Catch a concert at this iconic venue, where the acoustics and scenery are unmatched.

2. Amsterdam, Netherlands – The Classic Cannabis Capital

Amsterdam has long been synonymous with legal weed, but its charming canals and historic architecture make it a visually stunning destination.

Why Visit?

  • Coffeeshop Culture: Enjoy high-quality strains in cozy, licensed cafés.
  • Canal Cruises: Float through the city’s waterways while indulging in a pre-rolled joint.
  • Tulip Fields: Just outside the city, the Keukenhof Gardens explode with color in spring—perfect for a high stroll.

3. Jamaica – Reggae, Beaches, and Ganja

Jamaica’s Rastafarian culture has always embraced cannabis, and now it’s fully legal for both medical and religious use.

Why Visit?

  • Beachside Lounging: Negril’s Seven Mile Beach is ideal for a sunset smoke session.
  • Blue Mountains: Hike through lush rainforests and visit coffee plantations where you can pair your bud with world-famous Jamaican coffee.
  • Bob Marley Museum: Pay homage to the king of reggae and learn about cannabis’s role in Jamaican culture.

4. Portland, Oregon – Hipster Vibes & Wild Coastlines

Oregon’s progressive cannabis laws and stunning natural beauty make Portland a must-visit.

Why Visit?

  • Columbia River Gorge: Waterfalls, hiking trails, and scenic drives—all enhanced by legal weed.
  • Cannabis-Infused Dining: Try THC-laced edibles at restaurants that cater to cannabis connoisseurs.
  • Cannon Beach: Famous for Haystack Rock, this coastal gem is perfect for a mellow beach day.

5. Barcelona, Spain – Cannabis Clubs & Mediterranean Views

While Spain hasn’t fully legalized cannabis, private cannabis clubs in Barcelona operate in a legal gray area, offering high-quality products to members.

Why Visit?

  • Beachfront Smoking: Barceloneta Beach is a great spot to enjoy a joint with sea views.
  • Park Güell: Gaudí’s surreal park is even more mesmerizing with a little herbal enhancement.
  • Montserrat Mountains: A short trip from the city, these jagged peaks offer breathtaking hikes.

Emerging Hotspots for Cannabis Tourism

Canada – From Coast to Coast

Since nationwide legalization in 2018, Canada has become a haven for cannabis lovers.

Top Picks:

  • Vancouver, BC: A mix of urban dispensaries and nearby nature (think Whistler’s slopes and Pacific coastline).
  • Banff National Park: Imagine smoking a joint by glacial lakes surrounded by the Canadian Rockies.

Uruguay – South America’s Pioneer

Uruguay was the first country to fully legalize cannabis, and its laid-back beaches are perfect for a chill getaway.

Why Visit?

  • Punta del Este: A glamorous beach town where you can legally enjoy cannabis by the shore.
  • Montevideo’s Cannabis Clubs: Join a local club and sample Uruguayan-grown strains.

Tips for Traveling with Cannabis

Before you pack your bags (and your stash), keep these in mind:
- Check Local Laws: Even in legal areas, public consumption may be restricted.
- Don’t Cross Borders: Transporting weed internationally is illegal—buy locally.
- Respect Nature: Always clean up after yourself and avoid smoking in fire-prone areas.

The world is opening up to cannabis-friendly travel, and with so many stunning destinations to choose from, the hardest part is deciding where to go first. Whether you’re chasing mountain highs, beachside bliss, or urban adventures, there’s a perfect spot waiting for you.
